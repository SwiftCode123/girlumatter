Basic Hygiene

  • Middle school girls and good hygiene habits | Menstrual hygiene: Change pads/tampons regularly | Wash hands before/after handling products | Dispose of used items properly

  • Skin care: Wash face twice daily with mild cleanser | Avoid touching face excessively | Use oil-free, non-comedogenic moisturizers

  • Deodorant: Use during increased sweating Proper application and suitable products; Hair care: Shampoo and condition regularly Tie hair back during activities

  • Oral hygiene: Regular brushing and flossing Replace toothbrush every 3 months Visit dentist regularly Hand hygiene: Wash hands before meals, after restroom, coughing, sneezing Thorough handwashing with soap and water

  • Personal items: Don't share towels, hairbrushes, makeup Prevent germ spread and infections Proper disposal: Dispose of trash, hygiene products in designated bins Especially sanitary items during menstruation Open communication: Discuss hygiene concerns/questions Supportive, open environment Early habits impact health and well-being

    • Being Social

  • Social development for middle school girls Participate in group activities: Clubs, sports teams for shared interests Interaction with like-minded peers Practice active listening: Pay attention, show interest Ask follow-up questions for deeper talks Cultivate empathy and kindness: Treat others respectfully Understand different perspectives

  • Learn conversation starters: Develop ice-breaker questions Initiate conversations with new people Role-play social situations: Practice introductions, conflict handling Boost confidence for real interactions Embrace inclusive behavior: Include others in activities Create a welcoming atmosphere

  • Display positive body language: Maintain eye contact, smile, gestures Convey openness and friendliness Organize social events: Host playdates, study sessions, gatherings Strengthen social skills and friendships

  • Tips to enhance social skills: Encourage group activities like clubs or sports teams aligned with interests. Teach attentive listening and asking follow-up questions. Emphasize kindness, empathy, and respect. Develop conversation starters for new people. Role-play social situations to boost confidence. Teach inclusive behavior and welcoming attitudes. Explain positive body language: eye contact, smiling, gestures. Organize playdates or study sessions to foster friendships. Guide conflict resolution and compromise. Lead by example with healthy social behavior. Discuss online etiquette in the digital age.

    • Want to talk to someone?

  • Appear Approachable: Maintain positive body language, smile, and create a welcoming vibe. Identify Common Interests: Use shared hobbies or activities to start conversations. Begin with a Greeting: Start with a simple and friendly "Hello" or "Hi." Ask Open-Ended Questions: Encourage detailed responses by posing thought-provoking questions.

  • Listen Actively: Pay close attention and show genuine interest in the speaker. Give Sincere Compliments: Break the ice by offering genuine compliments. Share Personal Experiences: Make the conversation engaging by sharing relevant personal stories. Practice Empathy: Understand and connect with the speaker's emotions and viewpoint.

  • Avoid Sensitive Topics: Stay away from controversial subjects, especially early on. Respect Boundaries: Be mindful of personal space and avoid pushing uninterested individuals. Use Appropriate Humor: Introduce light-hearted jokes to ease tension and foster a relaxed atmosphere.
